# Municipal Party-Affiliation Audit

## Conclusion

Toronto mayoral results must remain candidate-based rather than party-based.
The City of Toronto explicitly states that there are no political parties at
the local level of government. The official 2023 mayoral ballot and certified
result declaration list candidate names without political-party labels.

Olivia Chow's former NDP affiliation is historically correct for her service
as a federal Member of Parliament. The House of Commons records her NDP
affiliation for that federal office through March 12, 2014. It is not an
official party label for her 2023 Toronto mayoral candidacy.

Assigning mayoral votes to NDP, Liberal, Conservative, or another party based
on a candidate's past office, endorsements, public reputation, or inferred
ideology would create a derived classification that is absent from the ballot.
It would also require subjective decisions for the other 101 candidates.
Those votes therefore remain official non-party candidate votes.

## Map Decision

- Provincial and federal layers support party vote-share maps.
- The municipal layer supports candidate vote-share maps.
- Municipal hover details report leading candidate shares and vote estimates.
- No inferred municipal party-vote field is added to the election or
  interpolation datasets.
